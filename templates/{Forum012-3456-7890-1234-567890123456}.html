How the Forum Works
Forums are the categories of different discussions you want your site visitors to participate in. Only you can define Forums. Once these are defined, either you or your site visitors can start Topics within any particular forum.

For example, if you created a Forum called "Recipes" a site visitor might come in and start a Topic within that forum called "Cheese Omelet". At least, that would be the Subject (or Title) of the Topic. Then, in the Topic's post box, he would input the actual ingredients and instructions for making a Cheese Omelet. Once there are Topics within a Forum, people can begin to post Replies. Replies are comments posted in direct response to a Topic. So let's say that Cheese Omelet Topic called for 2 eggs, but someone tried the recipe and used 3 eggs and it came out better. That person would then post a Reply to the Topic and discuss how he made the recipe better.

An infinite number of Topics are allowed within any Forum, and users may post an infinite number of Replies to each Topic. This is how a user community grows and interacts with each other on the web, thanks to Forums like this one.
